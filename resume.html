How to Make a Good Resume

With your resume, you want to stand out. On average, you have about 10-20 seconds to do that. Make those few seconds count.

Header- Start your resume off with the most important information: your name, number, email, and website if you have one. A permanent mailing address is appropriate but optional and not entirely necessary.

Margins- Make your margins about 1/2 to 1 inch on all sides just in case your possible employer wants to print it out. You don't want important information to be left out.

Fonts and Font Size- The font and its size matters. Times New Roman is usually the go-to font but standing out with that will be a challenge. Use serif or sans-serif fonts to be professional but also different. Fonts such as Calibri, Cambria, Georgia, Verdana, and Helvetica are ideal. Also, keep in mind that some fonts are bigger than others. Arial 12 is bigger than Times New Roman 12. Your resume should be legible. Don't sacrifice legibility in order to get everything on the page.

Experience/Qualifications- When listing your work history, don't forget to list who you worked for and for how long, the most recent being on top. Be sure to list the title you held and a quick bulleted list of your responsibilities and/or duties.

For more in depth instructions, visit http://rockportinstitute.com/resumes/ or http://theinterviewguys.com/how-to-make-a-resume-101/.
